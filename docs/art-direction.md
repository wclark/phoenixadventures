# Art Direction: Falconrise Keep

This setting is inspired by public references for Rincon Valley Middle School and Santa Rosa Accelerated Charter, but it is a simplified fantasy adventure map rather than a real campus map.

## Public reference cues

- Rincon Valley Middle School official site: shared public school identity, "Home of the Falcons," and the 4650 Badger Road, Santa Rosa address. Source: https://www.rinconvalleymiddleschool.com/
- Santa Rosa Accelerated Charter official site: confirms the charter school at the same public address. Source: https://sracs.srcschools.org/our-school
- California Department of Education profiles: confirm public directory details for both schools. Sources: https://www.cde.ca.gov/schooldirectory/details?cdscode=49709206060271 and https://www.cde.ca.gov/schooldirectory/details?cdscode=49709200102533
- Public map/photo listings: broad exterior cues such as a shared campus setting, athletic field, track-like open ground, courts, and spacious grounds. Sources: https://www.mapquest.com/us/california/rincon-valley-middle-school-12105666 and https://www.homes.com/school/santa-rosa-ca/rincon-valley-middle-school/mbclcvth8jrv7/
- Official SRACS exterior photo: low single-story school building, pale vertical siding, deep roof overhang, walkway railing, trees, and open paved lot. Source: https://resources.finalsite.net/images/f_auto,q_auto,t_image_size_4/v1687885583/srcschoolsorg/nwgiw08buwj5z3obn3hp/SRAcceleratedCharterSchoolattheRinconValleyMiddlecampus.jpg
- OpenStreetMap/Overpass geometry: broad campus boundary, running track, basketball/tennis courts, ball fields, and parking-area relationship. Source data from OpenStreetMap under ODbL via Overpass API.

## Transformation Rules

- Do not recreate entrances, access routes, fences, interior layouts, or parking circulation details.
- Keep the map simplified, schematic, renamed, and unsuitable for real-world navigation.
- Translate real-world cues into genre equivalents:
  - Shared campus becomes a walled ruin called Falconrise Keep.
  - Main school building masses become hall wings surrounding a central courtyard.
  - The smaller SRACS building becomes SRAC'S with a separate wagon yard.
  - Athletic grounds become a tournament track and overgrown commons.
  - Courts become cracked training yards.
  - Falcon identity becomes heraldry and carved stone motifs.
  - Phoenix identity becomes tavern signage, lanterns, and warm red-gold accents.
  - Santa Rosa foothill vegetation becomes valley oak, laurel, dry grass, and ash-vine growth.
